Chameleon

  2. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameloens
  3. Most chameloens have a prehensile tail that they use to warp around tree branches

Teddy bear

  1. After the Titanic sank in 1912, German toy company Steiff created 500 teddy bears to honor the victims
  2. A Canadian soldier bought a black bear cub from a hunter during World War I, and the animal became a pet and mascot for his troop
  3. The smallest commercially available stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall, made by South African “microbear” maker Cheryl Moss, whose largest creations are just taller than half an inch.

Panda

  1. Pandas grow from tiny to massive in a few short years
  2. Sure, panda facts are usually light-hearted and fun but don’t be misled by the cute faces and cuddly-looking fur
  3. Adult pandas don’t spend much time interacting with each other, and they’re generally quiet animals.

Cicada

  1. There are around 3,000 cicada species, according to National Geographic, so they vary in size from 0.75 to 2.25 in (2.2 to 5.5 cm) long.
  2. The buzzing sound they make is a mating call.
  3. While no species of cicada are endangered, a few are at risk, according to the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN).
